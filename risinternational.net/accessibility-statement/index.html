1. Our Commitment to Accessibility

At RIS International Services, we believe that the web should be accessible to everyone, regardless of their abilities or disabilities. We are committed to providing an inclusive digital experience that enables all users to access our services and information effectively.

Accessibility is not just a compliance requirement for us—it's a fundamental principle that guides our design and development processes. We strive to create digital solutions that are usable by people with diverse abilities, including those who use assistive technologies.

Our Promise: We are dedicated to continuously improving the accessibility of our website and services, ensuring equal access and opportunity for all users.

2. Standards and Guidelines

Our website aims to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 at the AA level. These guidelines explain how to make web content more accessible to people with disabilities.

WCAG 2.1 AA

We follow the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines 2.1 at the AA conformance level, which covers a wide range of recommendations for making web content more accessible.

Section 508

Our accessibility efforts also align with Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act, ensuring compliance with federal accessibility standards.

ADA Compliance

We strive to meet the requirements of the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) to ensure equal access to our digital services.

International Standards

We also consider international accessibility standards including EN 301 549 and ISO/IEC 40500 to ensure global accessibility compliance.

4. Testing and Evaluation

We regularly test our website's accessibility using various methods and tools to ensure compliance and usability:

Automated Testing

Regular automated scans using tools like axe, WAVE, and Lighthouse to identify accessibility issues.

Manual Testing

Human evaluation including keyboard navigation, screen reader testing, and usability assessments.

User Testing

Testing with real users, including people with disabilities, to gather feedback and improve accessibility.

Testing Schedule

Regular Testing

  • • Weekly automated scans
  • • Monthly manual reviews
  • • Quarterly comprehensive audits

Special Testing

  • • Before major releases
  • • After significant updates
  • • Following user feedback
